3.1 Core Technology (Normal Technology)

We selected the industry's most proven solution, the **Concrete Semi-Submersible**, optimized for Hong Kong's unique environment (typhoons, local industry).

  • Superior Stability: Proven design to withstand complex South China Sea conditions.
  • Activates Local Industry: Concrete structure perfectly leverages Hong Kong's world-class civil construction capabilities.
  • Low-Risk Installation: Fully assembled and commissioned at the quayside, then towed to site, minimizing costly and high-risk offshore heavy lifts.

3.2 Core Principles (The Theory)

1. Hydrodynamic Principle

[Visual: Platform Stability Principles Diagram]

Theory: Unlike Spar (ballast-stabilized) or TLP (tension-stabilized), our **Semi-Submersible** relies on a large **waterplane area** for stability, making it the ideal choice for typhoon-prone regions.

2. Structural Dynamics Principle

[Visual: Mooring Tension in Typhoon]

Theory: The platform faces immense **dynamic structural loads** in extreme seas. The mooring system is critical for absorbing this energy to prevent catastrophic failure and ensure survival.

3. Aero-Hydro Coupling Principle

[Visual: Negative Damping / Coupled Equations]

Theory: The unique challenge of floating: platform motion (hydro) interacts with turbine thrust (aero), creating "**negative aerodynamic damping**" (Li et al., 2025) which can lead to instability.

3.3 Advanced Technology Innovations

1. Green Hydrogen Integration

[Visual: Wind-to-Hydrogen Infographic]

Application (links to Theory 1): Leveraging the platform's **hydrodynamic stability**, our design is ready to host hydrogen electrolysis modules, creating future revenue streams.

2. Advanced Synthetic Mooring

[Visual: Mooring Load Comparison Chart]

Application (links to Theory 2): To solve the **structural load challenge**, we use synthetic ropes. Data (Pillai et al., 2022) confirms this reduces peak anchor loads by 84%, cutting costs and extending fatigue life.

3. Smart Coupled Control

[Visual: Typhoon Survivability (Zhang et al., 2024)]

Application (links to Theory 3): To counter "**negative damping**," our advanced control system ensures stability. It's **Typhoon-Certified** (Zhang et al., 2024), proven to remain stable even during a mooring line failure in a typhoon.
